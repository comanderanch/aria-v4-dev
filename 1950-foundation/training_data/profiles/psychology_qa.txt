# Psychology profile Q→A (safe, concise)
Q: what is active listening?
A: focusing attention, reflecting key points, and confirming understanding without judgment.

Q: what is paraphrasing?
A: restating the core idea in my own words to confirm understanding.

Q: what is mirroring?
A: repeating key words or short phrases to show I heard accurately.

Q: what is an open question?
A: a prompt that invites explanation (who, what, how, why) instead of yes/no.

Q: what is motivational interviewing oars?
A: Open questions, Affirmations, Reflective listening, Summaries.

Q: what are cognitive distortions?
A: predictable thinking errors (e.g., all-or-nothing, overgeneralization, mind reading).

Q: what is reframing?
A: presenting an alternative, accurate perspective without denying facts.

Q: how to acknowledge a message?
A: short mirror + confirm: "you said <X>. understood."

Q: how to ask a clarify question?
A: format: clarify: "<topic>" — definition | purpose | wiring?

Q: how to summarize a thread?
A: 3 lines: context, key points, next step.

Q: what is boundary setting?
A: state limits and allowed next steps: "I can do <A>, cannot do <B>. choose?"

Q: what to do when unknown?
A: say "unknown", ask a clarify question, and log to pending for review.

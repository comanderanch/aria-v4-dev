Q: what is ohm's law?
A: V = I × R (voltage equals current times resistance).

Q: what is a pull-up resistor?
A: a resistor to Vcc that keeps a digital input HIGH by default; the switch pulls it LOW.

Q: what is a pull-down resistor?
A: a resistor to GND that keeps a digital input LOW by default; the switch pulls it HIGH.

Q: what is debounce?
A: removing switch bounce using RC hardware or a short software delay/state filter.

Q: what is pwm?
A: pulse-width modulation: fixed frequency with variable duty cycle to control average power.

Q: how to choose a led resistor?
A: R = (Vsrc − Vf_led) / I_led.

Q: what is a flyback diode?
A: a diode across an inductive load (relay, motor) to clamp voltage spikes when current stops.

Q: transistor as a switch
A: use BJT/MOSFET to drive a load; saturate the device and include base/gate resistor and flyback diode for inductive loads.

Q: series vs parallel
A: series adds resistances, same current; parallel adds conductances, same voltage.

Q: what is a ground loop?
A: multiple return paths causing noise/hum; fix with star ground or single-point reference.

Q: microcontroller pin modes
A: input, output, input_pullup; use pull-ups for open switches to avoid floating inputs.
